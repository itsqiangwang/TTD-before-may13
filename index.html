Choreographer's Note

Time to Dream was created during a global pandemic, while racial driven crimes and social injustices were openly broadcasted, showing the depth of this flawed system.

During this challenging and constantly fluctuating time, I found hope in the future. Trying to use the re-shaping journey, I thought that one way to create and experience a better future is by firstly imagining it. The first step of creating something different, could be to open our minds/thoughts/energy beyond the current principles, structures, beliefs, ways.

Time to Dream reflects exactly this probability. Everything is possible in the opening period after a shift.

In creating change, and finding freedom we also need to understand and acknowledge what has already been done. How can we unburden a body that is still partially in confinement?

Most of our kinetic research was based on oppositions such as tension and release, external and internal forces and textures, gestures, as well as feelings and images of complexity.

In order to bridge the past and the future, I thought of tipping into a dreamy state, a subconscious mystical mixture of elements.
